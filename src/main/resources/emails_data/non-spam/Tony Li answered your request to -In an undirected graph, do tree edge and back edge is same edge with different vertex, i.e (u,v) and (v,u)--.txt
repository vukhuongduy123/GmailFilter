Tony Li answered the question "In an undirected graph, do tree edge and back edge is same edge with different vertex, i.e (u,v) and (v,u)?":

"In an undirected graph, there’s no such thing as a ‘back edge’. An edge simply has two vertices. (u,v) and (v,u) are just two different names for the same edge."

In an undirected graph, do tree edge and back edge is same edge with different vertex, i.e (u,v) and (v,u)?

Written 16m ago


In an undirected graph, there’s no such thing as a ‘back edge’. An edge simply has two vertices. (u,v) and (v,u) are just two different names for the same edge.
